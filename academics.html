Islamic Courses

Jamia Nooria offers a comprehensive curriculum blending classical Islamic sciences with modern studies — nurturing scholars who are grounded in tradition and prepared for contemporary life.

Deeniyat

Duration: Foundation level

A foundational course in Islamic basics covering three core pillars:

  • Aqeedah — Islamic beliefs and creed according to Ahle Sunnat Wa Jama'at
  • Ibadat — Acts of worship including Salah, Sawm, Zakat, and Hajj
  • Akhlaq — Islamic conduct, etiquette, and moral character

This course forms the bedrock of every student's Islamic education at Jamia Nooria.

Nazira Wa Tajweed

Duration: 1–2 years (based on prior knowledge)

Qur'an reading with proper pronunciation rules (Tajweed). Students learn:

  • Correct articulation of Arabic letters (Makhaarij)
  • Rules of elongation (Madd) and stopping (Waqf)
  • Ghunna, Idgham, Iqlab and other Tajweed rules
  • Fluent recitation of the complete Qur'an
Hifz e Quraan

Duration: 3–5 years (depending on individual progress)

Full Qur'an memorization program under qualified Hafizas. The program includes:

  • Individual attention from dedicated female Hafiza teachers
  • Daily revision (Sabaq, Sabaqi, Manzil system)
  • Tajweed integrated throughout memorization
  • Regular oral examinations to ensure retention

Graduates receive the distinguished title of Hafiza upon completion.

Qira't

Duration: 1–2 years

The art of Qur'anic recitation with melody and maqamat. Students learn:

  • Classical Arabic maqamat (melodic modes) for recitation
  • Tarteel — measured, beautiful recitation
  • Recitation styles of renowned Qaris
  • Performance in Qira't competitions (Musabaqah)
Dars e Nizami (Aalimah / Fazilat)

Duration: 8 years (Aalimah) + 2 years (Fazilat/Post-graduation)

The classical Islamic graduation program — the crown of the curriculum. Subjects covered:

  • Tafseer — Qur'anic exegesis (Ibn Katheer, Jalalayn)
  • Hadith — Sciences of Prophetic traditions (Bukhari, Muslim, Tirmidhi)
  • Fiqh — Islamic jurisprudence according to Hanafi school
  • Usool al-Fiqh — Principles of Islamic law
  • Arabic Literature & Grammar — Nahw, Sarf, Balagha
  • Mantiq — Logic and Islamic philosophy
  • Aqeedah — Advanced theology

Post-graduation (Fazilat) offers advanced specialization in Hadith and Fiqh. Graduates earn the title Aalimah or Fazilah.
